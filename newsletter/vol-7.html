This Week's Thesis

We Are No Longer in the AI Hype Era. We Are in the AI Infrastructure Era.

Since our last issue on May 11th, the ground shifted. Anthropic just eclipsed OpenAI's valuation, a sitting President signed a sweeping AI executive order, Microsoft deployed agents that literally use your computer like a human, and Workday dropped data showing that Gen Z workers are 12 times more likely than Gen X to feel completely disconnected from their colleagues because of AI workflows.

The bubble skeptics lost their argument. The efficiency zealots may be winning the productivity war while losing the culture one. And somewhere in the middle, the founders who understand system architecture — not just prompt engineering — are pulling away from the pack.

Finance / Venture Capital

Anthropic Just Dethroned OpenAI — And Wall Street Bankrolled the Whole Thing

On May 28th, Anthropic closed a $65 billion Series H, pushing its post-money valuation to $965 billion — officially surpassing OpenAI as the most valuable AI company in the world. The round was led by Altimeter, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, and Sequoia, with Amazon contributing $5 billion of the total. That same week, Anthropic announced a $47 billion annualized revenue run rate, up from $10 billion last year. The driver? Enterprise adoption of Claude Code and Claude for business operations.

As a bonus reveal, Anthropic also filed confidentially for an IPO — signaling that the AI era is no longer a startup story. It's a public markets story.

02
Policy / Regulation

The White House Drew Its Line: Innovation First, Mandatory Licensing Never

On June 2nd, President Trump signed "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" — the most significant federal AI action of the year. The order creates a voluntary 30-day review window for frontier AI developers to give the government early access before broader release, while explicitly barring any mandatory licensing or pre-clearance requirements for AI models.

Context matters: earlier drafts had a 90-day mandatory review. The administration walked it back after pushback from industry — choosing innovation velocity over regulatory friction. The order was directly triggered by capabilities demonstrated in Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview, which showed unprecedented ability to identify cyber vulnerabilities.
News
Voluntary framework, 30-day review window, zero mandatory licensing. Signed June 2, 2026.
Insight
America's AI policy is clear: build fast, prosecute bad actors, don't stifle builders with red tape.
Takeaway
For US founders, the regulatory window is wide open. The government is betting on innovation over control — for now.
03
Platform / Enterprise Tech

Your Software Just Became Obsolete. Microsoft Made It Official.

In May 2026, Microsoft's Copilot Studio made computer-using agents generally available — meaning enterprises can now deploy AI agents that navigate desktops, scroll, click, and enter data across any app exactly like a human, with zero custom API integrations required. At the same time, remote Model Context Protocol (MCP) server support launched in preview, letting agents plug into private databases and enterprise tools through a single open standard — think of it as USB-C for AI.

The downstream consequence? Enterprise buyers are aggressively rejecting traditional per-seat SaaS pricing. Why pay for 50 licenses when one agent handles the workflow? High-growth startups are pivoting entirely to outcome- or consumption-based pricing — charging per execution, per revenue generated, per problem solved.
News
Computer-using agents GA in Copilot Studio. MCP becomes the enterprise standard for AI tool integration.
Insight
The "per seat" SaaS model is dying. Background agents don't need seats. They need execution fees.
Takeaway
If your SaaS product charges per user, you have 12-18 months to reprice or reposition before the market does it for you.

Culture / Workplace Research

AI Killed Burnout. It's Also Killing Connection. Here's the Data.

Workday's Human Connection Workplace Index (May 27, 2026) surveyed 2,150 workers actively using AI across seven countries. The headline: AI is reducing burnout — but it's creating something arguably worse. One-third of workers say their weekly workplace interactions have become purely transactional. Gen Z is 12 times more likely than Gen X to report feeling completely disconnected from colleagues since adopting agentic workflows. And 37% of employees now turn to AI — not colleagues — for companionship and advice.

Here's the part that should stop every founder cold: 20% of Gen Z workers took time off this year due to loneliness or isolation. Not burnout from too much work — but disconnection from too little human contact.
News
Workday Index: 33% purely transactional interactions. Gen Z 12x more isolated. 37% use AI for companionship.
Insight
Efficiency without intentional human design isn't a win. It's a time-delayed culture collapse.
Takeaway
Build your AI infrastructure AND your human checkpoints. Trust is a moat. If automation erodes it, you'll pay in attrition, not efficiency.
